Project background

Melbourne agency underwent a significant rebranding initiated by its owner, aiming to elevate their digital service offerings. After auditing their previous website, it was evident the site lacked distinct brand personality. Additionally, using a marketplace template restricted their uniqueness. Thus, we recommended a fully customized redesign to better reflect the brands unique character and strengthen market positioning.

The challenge

The client was particularly fond of a textured wall from his office and wanted to integrate it into the websites hero section. Initially, translating this texture digitally proved challenging as early wireframes didnt capture its essence or reflect the brands personality.

We experimented extensively in Photoshop, proposing creative concepts with storytelling elements. Ultimately, we leveraged this texture as a symbolic backdrop for the hero section, creating a conversational atmosphere that mirrors interactions between the client and their prospective customers.

User Experience Journey

Our primary goal was to create an intuitive and streamlined user experience. Recognizing that the homepage serves as the primary gateway for various user journeys, we strategically structured it to effortlessly direct visitors towards Melbournes diverse services.

Rather than isolating each service into separate pages, as initially requested by the client, we proposed integrating all services directly onto the homepage. This approach simplifies navigation, improves readability, and boosts user engagement leading to higher conversion rates.

Key Requirements

A key requirement from the client was to visually distinguish each of their six service offerings through unique elements that align with the brands identity. We chose to focus on the 3D design service as a central theme, creating distinct 3D shapes for each service.

Colors were selected according to clear guidelines, inspired directly by the wall texture, to ensure cohesive branding across all visual elements. We thoroughly tested and validated these designs with Melbournes stakeholders, clearly communicating our rationale and demonstrating how the new visual identity aligns with their marketing strategy.

Process

We used Figma for all design work, and FigJam or Miro for brainstorming sessions and ideation. Weekly calls were scheduled to align on goals, share progress, and address any challenges or open questions.

Communication between the client and our team flowed seamlessly. Every idea and question raised during the process was carefully considered, ensuring no detail was overlooked and every perspective was acknowledged.

Melbournes website project was a great success, marked by efficient collaboration and enthusiastic client engagement. We successfully delivered a website that went live a week ahead of schedule, thanks to thorough planning and structured execution.
